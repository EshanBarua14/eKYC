01 Executive Summary

BFIU Circular No. 29 was issued on March 30, 2026 mandating all Bangladesh Insurance Companies and Capital Market Intermediaries (CMIs) to implement electronic KYC (eKYC) by December 31, 2026. Xpert Fintech Ltd. is building the first production-ready, multi-tenant eKYC SaaS platform for this segment.


As of April 27, 2026, 1,404 automated tests pass across 62 built modules. The core compliance logic — biometric session management, screening, risk grading, EDD, RBAC, audit trail — is functionally complete but not yet production-deployable due to infrastructure gaps, demo-mode integrations, and missing legal agreements.

02 Project Goal & Scope

The Problem

No production-ready, BFIU-compliant eKYC platform exists for Bangladesh's Insurance/CMI sector. Paper-based KYC is slow, error-prone, and non-compliant with Circular No. 29. The 9-month window (Apr–Dec 2026) is a first-mover opportunity.


Target Institutions

All Bangladesh Insurance Companies and Capital Market Intermediaries including stock dealers, stock brokers, portfolio managers, merchant bankers, securities custodians, and asset management companies.

Platform Deliverable

Multi-tenant SaaS eKYC platform on PostgreSQL schema-per-tenant isolation. Single Xpert instance serves 100+ institutions simultaneously with full data separation.


Regulation-as-Code: Every BFIU threshold, session limit, and screening trigger is an API-enforced executable rule — not a UI policy that can be bypassed.


Audit-First: Every state transition generates an immutable audit entry before response is sent. System cannot complete an action without recording it.
